# Hook brief (DocMap)

## The brief

Our content currently educates women about endometriosis. That's not our job. Our job is to make the navigation problem feel urgent, specific, and solvable — and then be the solution.

Every hook we write should pass this test: does it create a gap that only DocMap closes? If someone can read it, feel informed, and move on without ever needing us, it has failed.

## What we're moving away from

- Hooks that validate the problem in the abstract.
- Hooks that could have been written by any women's health account with no product behind them.
- Hooks that position us as educators rather than navigators.
- Anything that feels like it belongs in a patient leaflet.

## What we're moving toward

Hooks rooted in specific, real experiences — not types of experience.

- The humiliation of being referred to the wrong person for two years.
- The specific moment someone realised a better path existed and nobody had told them.
- The mild outrage of finding out the system had a shortcut they were never shown.

The voice is: someone who has been through it, is slightly pissed off, and is telling you something they wish they'd known sooner. Direct. Unhedged. Occasionally uncomfortable.

## The three modes that work

### One — make the navigation problem visceral and granular

Not "the system is failing you." The specific, named, countable ways it fails.

### Two — make the existence of a better path feel almost unfair

The emotion is not validation. It's mild outrage that this was available and nobody told them.

### Three — show a real before/after journey

At the level of a specific conversation or moment, not a summary.

## The unfair advantage

We have real patient conversations. The specificity has to come from those. A hook that could have been written by someone who has never spoken to an endometriosis patient is not good enough.

## Compatibility notes (how this fits DocMap’s broader marketing guidance)

This brief is directionally consistent with:

- `DOCMAP_CONTEXT.md`: DocMap is a **navigation + preparation** layer (not diagnosis, not medical advice). Hooks should highlight confusing journey moments DocMap helps resolve.
- `SCORING_AND_SELECTION.md`: carousels are a **utility engine** — clarity, saveability, and next steps win. Hooks should pull readers into *structured action* (track, prep, questions, what to do next).

To avoid conflicts, interpret “a gap only DocMap closes” as:

- A gap in **clarity, preparation, and navigation** (e.g. “how do I tell my story in 8 minutes?”, “what details matter?”, “who should I actually be referred to?”),
- Not a promise to diagnose, guarantee access, or replace clinicians.

Voice guardrails:

- “Slightly pissed off” should read as **direct and specific**, not fear-based, exaggerated, or salesy.
- We can be uncomfortable by naming real friction (dismissal, referrals, wasted time) while staying **calm, credible, and human**.
