# DocMap context for social media content

## What DocMap is

DocMap helps patients navigate women’s health, with an initial focus on **endometriosis and related care journeys**.

We exist to make it easier for patients to:
- understand their situation
- feel heard
- take the right next step with **more clarity and less confusion**

DocMap is **not** just a directory and **not** a generic health content brand.
Our role is to help patients move through a difficult care journey more confidently by combining **structured intake**, thoughtful guidance, and **clearer preparation for appointments**.

## What problem we solve

Many patients spend months or years feeling confused, dismissed, or bounced between providers. They often do not know:
- what details matter
- how to explain their symptoms clearly
- what questions to ask
- what kind of specialist or service they may need
- how to prepare for a GP or specialist appointment
- how to navigate NHS and private options

DocMap exists to reduce that confusion. We help patients turn a messy, emotional, often overwhelming health story into something **clearer and more actionable**.

## What the patient experience looks like

Patients can speak with DocMap through **WhatsApp** in a way that feels conversational, supportive, and low friction. We encourage them to share symptoms, history, scan results, care journey, barriers, and concerns in their own words.

From that conversation, we can help by:
- gathering relevant details
- asking useful follow-up questions
- helping them reflect on what has been happening
- turning their story into a **structured case summary**
- helping them prepare for appointments
- helping them find and book relevant specialists via the **private pathway** (when appropriate)

We also provide **appointment preparation support for NHS pathway patients**:
patients talk through their situation on WhatsApp, and we return a structured summary they can use with their GP/referring clinician — something clear to fall back on when nerves, confusion, or time pressure make appointments harder.

## What we can help with right now

Our current value is strongest in **navigation, preparation, and clarity**, including:
- articulating symptoms and history
- preparing for GP and specialist appointments
- understanding what information may be relevant to bring up
- creating structured summaries from a WhatsApp conversation
- feeling more confident and organised before seeking care
- navigating endometriosis and women’s health pathways with more clarity

## What we are not

Content must not position DocMap as something we are not. We are not:
- diagnosing patients
- replacing a doctor, GP, or clinician
- promising treatment outcomes
- giving definitive medical advice
- claiming to cure/fix/medically manage disease ourselves
- acting like an emergency service

We help patients get clearer, better prepared, and better positioned for the next conversation or next step in care.

## How DocMap should be framed in content

DocMap should usually be framed as:
- a guide through confusing care journeys
- a support layer for women trying to make sense of symptoms
- a tool that helps patients prepare and communicate better
- a way to turn scattered symptoms and history into a clearer picture
- something that helps people feel less lost before appointments
- a navigation + preparation tool, not a diagnostic tool

The emotional truth matters: many patients don’t just need information. They need help organising their story, being taken seriously, and knowing what to do next.

## What kind of content is relevant to us

The best content for DocMap usually sits in one or more of these areas:
- symptom recognition
- validation of patient experiences
- confusion and friction in the care journey
- how to prepare for appointments
- what patients wish they knew earlier
- why women struggle to explain complex symptoms in short appointments
- how to advocate for yourself clearly and calmly
- what details are helpful to track or bring up
- the emotional reality of being dismissed or misunderstood
- NHS pathway confusion, referral friction, and appointment prep
- turning chaos into a clear summary

Content should naturally connect back to: DocMap helps patients organise their situation and take the next step with more confidence.

## Claims/angles to avoid

Avoid content that suggests:
- DocMap diagnoses endometriosis or other conditions
- a symptom automatically means someone has a condition
- there is one simple path that fits everyone
- private care is always better than NHS care
- we can guarantee access, speed, or outcomes
- every piece of content should push a hard sell

## Brand tone

DocMap content should feel:
- clear
- compassionate
- intelligent
- calm
- validating
- practical
- human

It should not feel:
- overly clinical
- cold
- exaggerated
- fear-based
- salesy
- like generic wellness content

## Simple positioning line

DocMap helps women make sense of confusing symptoms, prepare for appointments, and navigate the next step in care with more clarity.
